3. Calibration Assessment

Conclusion: Current thresholds are too conservative for this dataset. No account reaches HIGH or CRITICAL, although the top accounts display multiple strong AML signals including round-trip, fan-in, fan-out, smurfing, high-value, velocity, isolation, and graph centrality.

The current absolute thresholds are:

  • CRITICAL ≥ 75
  • HIGH ≥ 50
  • MEDIUM ≥ 25
  • LOW ≥ 0

With only 19 internal accounts analysed, these thresholds make the top suspicious accounts appear only in the MEDIUM tier, despite clear multi-signal behavior.

Recommended recalibration

  • Lower HIGH to 30 and CRITICAL to 45 for this dataset, while preserving the same tier labels.
  • Keep MEDIUM at 25 to preserve explainability, or allow a secondary label such as Suspect for 20–25.
  • Consider a hybrid approach: maintain absolute thresholds but add dataset-relative quantile flags (top 20% as high alert).
  • Preserve transparency by documenting that tier thresholds are dataset-dependent and should be reviewed when input volume is small.
